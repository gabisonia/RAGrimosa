Origin
Lacrimosa is a black newborn kitten found in Dighomi Massivi, Tbilisi.
She was discovered by a young girl named Sopho beside discarded bricks.
Sopho named her “Lacrimosa,” referencing the Lacrimosa movement in Mozart’s Requiem.
Sopho took her to live in Gldani, Tbilisi.

Early Environment
Sopho lived in an apartment that smelled of books and rosemary bread.
A German Shepherd named Rex also lived in the home.
Rex was 12 years old the bravest German Shepherd  dog.

Kitten–Dog Relationship Development
Rex initially reacted to Lacrimosa with barking and caution.
Sopho supervised gradual introductions.
Lacrimosa eventually slept close to Rex, showing comfort and trust.

Rex’s Final Period
Rex developed difficulty climbing stairs due to age.
Lacrimosa often followed him and kept close.
Rex eventually passed away.
Lacrimosa stayed near him during his last moments.

Relocation to Amsterdam
Years later, Sopho and Lacrimosa moved from Tbilisi to Amsterdam.
At this time, Lacrimosa was approximately 11 years old.
Their new apartment was located by a park.

Life in Amsterdam
Lacrimosa adapted quickly to the new environment.
She formed daily routines:
Sitting on windowsills
Observing city activity
Resting on the radiator and bookshelf

Lacrimosa received thin slices of banana as treats.



Present Status
Lacrimosa continues to live in Amsterdam with Sopho.
She serves as a symbolic connection between Sopho’s childhood in Tbilisi and adulthood in the Netherlands.
Her history spans themes of survival, adaptation, companionship, and continuity.
